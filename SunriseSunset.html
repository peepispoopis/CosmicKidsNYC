What Makes the Sun Rise and Set?

The rising and setting of the Sun are phenomena caused by the rotation of the Earth on its axis. The Earth rotates once every 24 hours, causing different parts of the planet to face the Sun, creating day and night.

Sunrise occurs when the Earth's rotation brings your location into view of the Sun, and sunset happens when your location rotates away from the Sun. These times are influenced by the Earth's tilt and your geographic location.

    4. The Earth's Tilt and Seasons

    Because the Earth's axis is tilted by 23.5 degrees, the Sun appears to move across the sky in a slightly different path depending on the season. This is why the length of the day changes throughout the year, with longer days in the summer and shorter days in the winter.

    The Earth's tilt relative to the Sun affects the length and timing of sunrises and sunsets.

    3. When Does the Sun Rise and Set?

    The time of sunrise and sunset varies throughout the year and depends on your geographic location. In general, the Sun rises earlier and sets later during the summer months, and the reverse happens during the winter.

    The exact times of sunrise and sunset can be predicted based on the date and the observer's location on Earth. By knowing these times, people can plan activities such as hiking, photography, or observing astronomical events like solar eclipses.
